I wanted a portfolio that didn't just tell people what I do — but showed it.

So I built one from scratch. Not with a template, not with a drag-and-drop builder. Just an idea, a code editor, and a few late nights connecting the dots between quality engineering and front-end craft.

Everything you see — the 3D particle field on the hero, the smooth scroll sections, the dark gold aesthetic — was written by hand, piece by piece. The same way I approach test automation: intentional, structured, and focused on the experience at the other end.

The site pulls in my LinkedIn, GitHub, projects, and resume. The skills section covers what I actually work with day-to-day — Playwright, Selenium, TypeScript, AWS, CI/CD, AI-assisted QA. Not just a list, but a reflection of 13+ years of figuring things out.

I learned a ton building this — about animation, about layout, about how code and design meet. And honestly, I'm proud of how it turned out.
